My Elevator Pitch

History would suggest that product designers usually aren't capable of supporting development efforts through code once merge requests start to take flight. Meanwhile, engineers don't often find themselves with extra time to obsess over things like type scales and font weights.

That's where I fit into the equation. I've built my career around being the exception to both ideals. I find great solace between the two specialties, with the knowledge and ability to bridge that illustrious gap. Here's what you can expect on day one.

  • Design Debonair

    The world deserves beautiful and well thought out experiences. From niche SaaS apps to major financial media outlets that reach millions; my 16 year tenure designing across the web has been privy to it all.

  • Markup Magician

    The ability to write beautifully semantic, production ready HTML & CSS is something I enjoy and take pride in. Best yet, this frees up your full-stack engineers to do the work they actually want to do.

  • Figma Fanboy

    You're only as good as tools you use and I know design's newest kid on the block like the back of my hand. This means less time finagling with brushes and more time throwing paint at the canvas.

  • Systems Savant

    Our work thrives off finding patterns and making them reusable. With a history building design systems, my contributions have sped-up development time while enhancing user experiences as a result.

  • A11y Advocate

    Accessible code is good code. In the past, I've lead efforts to update existing products to meet ADA compliance; all while provoking new policies that align with the latest WCAG guidelines.

  • Responsive Rockstar

    With recent advancements in CSS, you shouldn't have to write your markup more than once. Abiding by this mindset, I've helped reduce technical overhead by removing the need for mobile specificity.

Total Expert

Jack of All Trades, Master of None

I've floated quite a bit from team to team in the past two years at Total Expert helping as needed with various design and front-end development tasks. While there are many items that could be included here, three projects in particular stand out with regard to my involvement and contributions.

Mobile Web CRM Proof of Concept

With this project, I was tasked with creating a working mobile web concept of our core CRM product to illustrate the benefits and drawbacks of responsive web solution when compared against an equilvalent native app. In total I had two weeks to design and develop a working prototype that was then presented to executive leadership.

This exercise remains one of the largest accomplishments in my time at Total Expert thus far. In the end, the decision ultimately favored hiring a third party vendor to develop a native Android and iOS application. However, this proved that with minimal effort and resources it is possible to reengineer the designs and codebase of our existing platform to offer a more expansive mobile web solution. An initative that has already found footing in future planning.

Project Responsibilities
UX/UI Design, Systems Engineer, Project Management, and Front-End Development

The Mosby Design System

Given past experiences with systems work at Dow Jones, one of my first tasks at Total Expert was to migrate the current library of application assets from Sketch over to Figma. Given the ehancements to component architecture that the latter supports, this required building components from the ground up. This also allowed us to refine the markup and styles that support each of these components along the way.

Project Responsibilities
UX/UI Design, Systems Engineer, Front-End Development, Documentation and Best Practices

It's a necessary evil to outline all the possible edge cases that a particular component like the multi-select dropdown can offer. In this case the multi-select dropdown can be presented in one of nine different ways. My responsibility with the design system was to ensure all nine of those deviations all presented themselves to the end user in the same manner on screen.

Documentation

Nobody wakes up in the morning excited to write documentation, but an accessible style guide is an important asset when creating any system. These guidelines allow for flexible usage given the circumstance while still feeling like part of a larger, more cohesive whole. Visual aids for what should and shouldn't be done for each situation aids in the understanding of how best to incorporate items.

The struggle became understanding the threshold for what should and shouldn't be editable for each individual asset. Nothing illustrates that idea more than the behemoth that is a data table component and the variety of information that can be presented in each indivdiual cell.
