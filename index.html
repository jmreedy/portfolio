Hello, World.

jmreedy is the online persona of a guy named Jason who enjoys making websites for a living. Currently, he finds himself building out a design system and component library for the folks over at Total Expert. In a previous life, he lead design and front-end development for MarketWatch; making it a more beautiful and engaging place to gather financial news.

Whilst not writing code or drawing pictures in Figma, he enjoys building virtual pinball machines in-between chasing after his three energetic children.

The Elevator Pitch

The way I see it, history would suggest that most product designers usually aren't capable of supporting development efforts once merge requests start to take flight. Meanwhile, engineers don't often find themselves with extra time to obsess over type scales and font weights. Lucky for you, I've built my career off being the expection to both. I find great solace between the two specialties with an ability to bridge the ilustrious gap.

  • Design Debonair

    The world deserves beautiful and well thought out experiences. From niche SaaS applications to major financial media outlets that reach millions; my 16 year tenure designing across the web has been privy to it all.

  • Markup Magician

    My ability to write beautifully scemantic, production ready HTML & CSS is something I enjoy and take pride in. Best yet, this frees up your full-stack engineers to do the work they actually want to do.

  • Figma Fanboy

    You're only ever as good as tools you use and I obsess over keeping up to date with design's new daily driver; learning how to squeeze every ounce of productity from what Figma has to offer.

  • Systems Savant

    This line of work thrives off of reusable patterns. I have a proven track record building successful design systems that speed up development time while simplifying end-user experiences as a result.

  • A11y Advocate

    I've championed previous efforts to update legacy products to meet or exceed WCAG 2.1 guidelines. All the while provoking new conversations for policies moving forward that coincide with ADA compliance.

  • Responsive Rockstar

    With CSS flexbox and grid, you should never write your markup more than once. Abiding by this mindset, I have cut previous legacy codebases in half by removing the need for a mobile counterpart
