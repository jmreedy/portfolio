Hello, World.

Whaddap fellow nerds, thanks for stopping by. I'll assume you've stumbled upon my humble collection of work because you're in the market for a web design/development unicorn. Either that, or you are my mother trying to explain what I do at a random church dinner party (hi Mom!)

I've always had an appreciation for design and the bug hit me at an early age; spending most of my free time designing things like video games, D&D dungeons, dream houses and beyond. As time progressed, I found myself falling more and more in love with the complicated art of creating functional user interfaces and the experiences that coincide with them. Which is where my focus sits today.

So why hire me; what makes me special? Carry on my intrepid inquisitor, let's venture down this path together.

The Elevator Pitch

The way I see it, history would suggest that most product designers usually aren't capable of supporting development efforts once merge requests start to take flight. Meanwhile, engineers don't often find themselves with extra time to obsess over things like type scales and font weights.

Lucky for you, I've built my career off being the exception to both. I find great solace between the two specialties with an ability to bridge that illustrious gap.

  • Design Debonair

    The world deserves beautiful and well thought out experiences. From niche SaaS apps to major financial media outlets that reach millions; my 16 year tenure designing across the web has been privy to it all.

  • Markup Magician

    My ability to write beautifully scemantic, production ready HTML & CSS is something I enjoy and take pride in. Best yet, this frees up your full-stack engineers to do the work they actually want to do.

  • Figma Fanboy

    You're only ever as good as tools you use and I obsess over keeping up to date with design's new daily driver; learning how to squeeze every ounce of productivity from what Figma has to offer.

  • Systems Savant

    This type of work thrives off of reusable patterns. With a proven track record building successful design systems, Iâ€™ve sped-up development time while simplifying user experiences as a result.

  • A11y Advocate

    I've championed previous efforts to update legacy products to meet or exceed WCAG 2.1 guidelines. All while provoking new conversations for policies moving forward that coincide with ADA compliance.

  • Responsive Rockstar

    With CSS flexbox and grid, you should never write your markup more than once. Abiding by this mindset, I have cut previous legacy codebases in half by removing the need for a mobile counterpart.
